Aunty Wu, Mother of 500 Kids
The Story of a Local Hero Who Saves 500+ Street Animals in Tianjin, China.
Ms.Wu is currently in the age of 60s, and has started to rescue and adopt cats and dogs in China since the age of 20s. Ever since she laied her eyes on the first dog that needed rescue in her neighborhood, she started her journey to building shelters and become the mother of 500 kids by scacrificing all personal time and belongings: including selling two houses during economically harsh times. The violations of pets’ welfare are demonstrated as abandon, physical violence, and improper treatments. Animals do not speak human languages, yet preventing them from being heard and protected.

About

Tiffany and Madeline build this website with the intention to tell the unheard stories of Aunty Wu and rescued dogs to the world on a digital platform, and thus reaching out to audience who can possibly extend the story via adoption. We also build this website collaboratively with multiple media exploration including coding, graphic design/digital drawing, and filmmaking to narrate the story.

"白儿白儿，Baibai" A doggie named "White White" in Chinese



Aged 16 in 2020, Baibai (White White) was rescued by Ms.Wu 15 years ago in front of a grocery store in China. Baibai (White White) was a stray dog, and her left eye was blind when Ms.Wu first spotted her. Ms.Wu adopted Baibai (White White) and brought her to the neighborhood that she rented as a place for shelter in 2005. Now, as one of the oldest dogs living with Ms.Wu, Baibai shares the deepest bond with her.

Painting illustrated by Madeline Feng | Story written/interviewd by Tiffany Wang

黄黄, "Yellow Yellow"



"黄黄" (Yellow yellow) and her brother was abandoned by people in the town where Ms.Wu's Shelter locates currently in 2020. When Ms. Wu first encountered “黄黄” (Yellow yellow), she's only about several-months-old with her brother puppy in a paper box. People in the town use Ms.Wu's sympathy for animals to evade their responsibilities to take care of their dogs' lives.

Milk Tea "奶茶"



In the summer of 2020, Ms.Wu encountered a dog trafficking truck on her way to a barber shop. Ms.Wu encountered Milk tea, a golden retriever that was treated poorly by her owner after giving birth to new puppies. Ms.Wu was urged to save Milktea from the trafficking truck, and purchased the dealer 600 CYN (85 dollars). "If I don't save her from the dealer, she (Milk tea) would be sold to restaurants and served as meals on the table.” Ms.Wu also said, "I named her Milk Tea because I like to drink milk tea, milk tea is cute!"
